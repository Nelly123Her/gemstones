Materials

Materials and techniques, in brief.

The “look” of an era is often the result of tools: alloying, stone-cutting, setting styles, and surface finishing.

What changes through time

Across centuries, jewelry evolves when materials become more available, tools improve, and tastes shift. A few technical themes return again and again.

Goldwork: casting, hammering, granulation, and filigree define much early technique.
Gem cutting: from cabochons to modern faceting, shaping how light is “held.”
Setting styles: bezel, prong, pavé, channel—each changes the silhouette and durability.
Care: hardness, toughness, and chemicals matter as much as beauty—especially for emeralds, pearls, and opals.
